Interest in IT

What is my interest in IT

IT is for solving problems. IT is a tool for make processes more efficient. With IT, you can transform a business process into action. You can remove the jams, smooth out the edges, automate, calibrate, refine and iterate. IT lets you focus on the content while it does the process.

When did my interest in IT start

I once built a database to collate the details of the lease agreements held by a government department. I did this as a simple inventory, which I needed. Before I had finished, I realised I could link this data to a calendar and generate a to-do list. I realised then I could use IT as my assistant, making my day easier and helping me keep on top of the jobs I need to do.

Was there a particular event or person that sparked my interest

Business is a world dominated by processes. I found myself sitting in front of a word processor that could do so much more. I hope it isn’t narcissistic to say, but it was my own inquisitiveness that sparked my interest.

Outline of my IT experience

I started with spreadsheets and databases. Then scripts in VB and python. When I began to study at RMIT, the bigger world of IT opened up for me. I have learnt to do lots of interesting things with IT, but my instincts are still to look for roles that IT can play in improving process design.

Why did I choose RMIT

My criteria for deciding how to study were these: It had to be online, flexibility in the load each semester and a focus on technical skills. OUA offered this environment. RMIT was the most technical offering OUA provided.

What I expected to learn at RMIT

I hope to graduate RMIT with a deep and broad knowledge of programming techniques so that I can choose between technologies and select the one that best fits the problem I face.

Project Idea

Project Overview

My project idea is to build an application that can help a pool shop provide profitable maintenance services to their clients.

Background

Pool shops usually have a maintenance arm that provides a full-service option, for clients that would prefer a pool man come around and keep the pool in tip-top condition for them.

The maintenance business is a dependable income-steam for the shop, but it could also easily be a big headache. The difference between these two alternate outcomes is scheduling, and that is what technology can do.

Details

Considering a single maintenance job, the profitability of the job is the price less the cost of inputs. The local market sets the price, so it is so what inflexible. The business must drive profitability through the control of its inputs.

There are three input costs:

  1. labour,
  2. travel (fuel and vehicle maintenance),
  3. pool chemicals.

I believe that travel time is more, or longer, than the time spent doing the job. This means labour and travel costs are closely related to eachother.

Summary

My idea is that scheduling and route planning can reduce the time spent travelling between jobs. With optimised routes, both the cost of labour and travel for each job decreases. Also, the number of tasks completed in a day can increase.

Motivation

Investigating a business and working out a technology solution that can be injected into the workflow to produce a tangible benefit is a primary professional interest for me.

Description

Aim: to optimise the routes pool maintenance technicians use to travel between their daily jobs list. Resources:

A database of information:

  • The total list of maintenance jobs
  • Job characteristics (Job type (classified into the chemicals required) , Estimated time (onsite), Due date, The time range for completion)
  • Client (Address)
  • Pool (Size, Construction type, Filter equipment type)
Tools and Technologies

Software:

  • backend database
  • a front-end web page for technicians
  • Route optimisation software
  • scripts to generate the weekly jobs list

Hardware:

  • a tablet to present routes and allow input
  • GPS unit for the vehicle
Skills Required

To complete the project, I will need skills in database implementation and front-end development. I will also need to know how to use the mapping API that will drive the route optimisation algorithm. There are several APIs available ( Optimoroute, myrouteonline ).

Outcome

User story for pool technician (Mr Pools).

At the start of the day, Mr Pools logs into his device and sees a list of jobs for the day. He can review a map of his itinerary for the day, showing the path he will take for the day. The app also provides a list of materials he will need throughout his day (based on the job type). Mr Pools loads his van and heads off following the onboard directions. When he arrives the first job the app records the start time and stops when he departs. At the end of the job, Mr Pools tick off it off as complete and records the actual chemicals he used (for billing). He amends the schedule for the time the job is due. While completing the maintenance, Mr Pools found that the filter cartage needs replacement, so he logs that job so it can be presented to the client and scheduled if it is approved.
