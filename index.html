Interest in IT

Before I had finished, I realised I could link this data to a calendar and generate a to-do list. I realised then I could use IT as my assistant, making my day easier and helping me keep on top of the jobs I need to do.

Was there a particular event or person that sparked my interest

Business is a world dominated by processes. I found myself sitting in front of a word processor that could do so much more. I hope it isn’t narcissistic to say, but it was my own inquisitiveness that sparked my interest.

Project Idea

Project Overview

My project idea is to build an application that can help a pool shop provide profitable maintenance services to their clients.

Background

Pool shops usually have a maintenance arm that provides a full-service option, for clients that would prefer a pool man come around and keep the pool in tip-top condition for them.

The maintenance business is a dependable income-steam for the shop, but it could also easily be a big headache. The difference between these two alternate outcomes is scheduling, and that is what technology can do.

Details

Considering a single maintenance job, the profitability of the job is the price less the cost of inputs. The local market sets the price, so it is some-what inflexible. The business must drive profitability through the control of its inputs.

There are three input costs:

  1. labour,
  2. travel (fuel and vehicle maintenance),
  3. pool chemicals.

I believe that travel time is as much, if not more, than the time spent doing the job. This means labour and travel costs are closely related to eachother.

Summary

My idea is that scheduling and route planning can reduce the time spent travelling between jobs. With optimised routes, both the cost of labour and travel for each job decreases. Also, the number of tasks completed in a day can increase.

Motivation

Investigating a business and working out a technology solution that can be injected into the workflow to produce a tangible benefit is a primary professional interest for me.

Description

Aim: to optimise the routes pool maintenance technicians use to travel between their daily jobs list.

Please read the user story written in the outcome section below. The user story has helped me discover how the application will function, but it is just one story. I see two users for the start-up phase of the project, being: the pool technician (Mr Pools) and the shop administrator (Ms Spa). Ms Spa’s job is to organise Mr Pools. She will need access to the system to add jobs, adjust priorities and communicate with clients. Ms Spa would also use the system to complete billing. She does not need the mapping/route facility. The third user – an enhancement – would be the client. They might log in to undertake tasks such as: see their jobs, pay bills, approve new work.

The project is a web application with three significant parts. It requires a backend database and a front-end content management system. It will also need a sophisticated algorithm to produce the technician’s daily route.

The front-end would have to be GPS enabled so that it can interact with the routing/mapping functionality. Essentially it is a jobs list and a route display. The front end would also report back time in progress, so it acts as an activity logger. It would have basic stock inventory functionality. And some billing functionality.

The back-end database stores data and requires user group authorisation. The data needed includes three tables: job characteristics (time to complete, materials, due date, due date flexibility, job priority), property (the pool's location), client info.

The route optimisation algorithm will work with the data stored in the database. It will produce an ordered jobs list that is passed to a mapping API. Although I would expect it to work on the fly, I would also expect it to plan each week. Looking at the job due in the week, it would lump them geographically to reduce travel between jobs. The algorithm should make minor adjustments through the week in response to work added to the schedule in an ad-hoc way.

Ms Spa would require the capacity to do long-range route optimisation. She could look far into the future, say 12 months, ahead. Then she might tweak the projected jobs list to get more optimisation out of the system; a scenario analyses functionality.

Tools and Technologies

Software:

  • backend database
  • a front-end web page for technicians
  • Route optimisation software
  • scripts to generate the weekly jobs list

Hardware:

  • a tablet to present routes and allow input
  • GPS unit for the vehicle
Skills Required

To complete the project, I will need skills in database implementation and front-end development. I will also need to know how to use the mapping API that will drive the route optimisation algorithm. There are several APIs available ( Optimoroute, myrouteonline ).

Outcome

User story for pool technician (Mr Pools).

At the start of the day, Mr Pools logs into his device and sees a list of jobs. He can review a map of his itinerary, showing the path he will travel for the day. The app also provides a list of materials he will need (based on the job type). Mr Pools loads his van and heads off following the onboard directions. When he arrives at the first job the app records the start time (and stops when he departs). At the end of the job, Mr Pools ticks it complete and records the actual chemicals he used (for billing). He updates when the job will next be due. While completing the maintenance, Mr Pools noticed that the filter cartage needs replacing. He records this as a future job so it can be presented to the client and scheduled if it is approved.

Mr Pools jumps back in the van, selects the next job and a map displays. He has a drink of water, turns up the radio and drives off.
